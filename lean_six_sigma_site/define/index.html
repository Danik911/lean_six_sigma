Phase Overview

The Define phase is the first step in the DMAIC methodology. During this phase, we identified and documented the problem, established the project scope, and set clear goals for improvement. The following documents outline the key components of our Define phase.

Problem Statement

Problem Statement

SimplePharma experiences inventory discrepancies between system records and physical counts affecting 18% of inventory items. These discrepancies lead to:

  • $125,000 in annual financial losses
  • 12% increase in emergency orders
  • Regulatory compliance risks under FDA guidelines
  • Customer dissatisfaction due to order fulfillment delays

The problem affects all warehouse sections but is most acute in high-value pharmaceutical areas and impacts multiple stakeholders including Operations, Finance, and Regulatory Compliance teams. The project aims to reduce inventory discrepancies by at least 50% within six months.

The Problem Statement clearly defines the business challenge, its impact, and quantifies the opportunity for improvement, providing clear direction for the project.

Define Phase Outcomes

Through the Define phase, we established that inventory discrepancies at SimplePharma are causing significant operational and compliance issues. The project aims to reduce inventory discrepancies by at least 50% within six months, improving operational efficiency and regulatory compliance.

The process mapping revealed several potential problem areas, including manual data entry, lack of standardized procedures, and communication gaps between departments. These insights guided our focus for the measurement phase.
