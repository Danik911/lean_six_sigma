Control Phase

Phase Overview

The Control phase ensures that the improvements implemented during the Improve phase are sustained over time. We established monitoring systems, standardized processes, and created control mechanisms to prevent regression to previous performance levels.

Statistical Process Control

Control Charts Implementation

We implemented a robust Statistical Process Control (SPC) system to monitor critical quality characteristics of the inventory process. Our SPC approach includes:

  • P-Charts: For monitoring the percentage of inventory items with discrepancies
  • I-MR Charts: For tracking the magnitude of discrepancies when they occur
  • Moving Range Charts: For identifying unusual patterns in process variation

Control limits were established based on the improved process performance, and any points outside these limits trigger an investigation and corrective action.

Key Control Mechanisms

Statistical Process Control

Implemented control charts to monitor inventory accuracy metrics with clearly defined control limits. Any out-of-control points trigger immediate investigation and corrective action.

Standard Operating Procedures

Developed detailed SOPs for all inventory management processes, with visual work instructions at key workstations to ensure consistent application.

Competency Management

Established an ongoing training and certification program to ensure all staff maintain required skill levels for inventory management tasks.

Equipment Maintenance

Implemented predictive maintenance for scanners and other inventory equipment, with automated alerts for calibration schedules.

Daily Management

Established daily huddles with visual management boards to review key metrics and highlight any emerging issues.

Audit Program

Created a robust audit schedule with randomized checks to verify that processes are being followed correctly.

Lessons Learned and Next Steps

Key learnings from this project that will inform future improvement initiatives:

  • Early stakeholder engagement is critical for successful implementation - Involving warehouse staff from the beginning led to higher adoption rates and better solution design.
  • Visual management tools significantly enhanced adoption of new processes - Simple visual cues and status indicators were more effective than detailed written procedures.
  • Technology solutions must be backed by robust standard procedures - Technology alone wasn't sufficient; it needed to be supported by well-defined processes and training.
  • Regular gemba walks by leadership reinforce importance of adherence to standards - When managers regularly observed the process, staff maintained higher levels of compliance.

The project team has identified additional opportunities for process improvement in related areas, which will be addressed in future Lean Six Sigma projects.
